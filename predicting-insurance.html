Predicting Insurance Costs with Linear Regression

Business Problem: Health insurance providers need accurate cost predictions to set fair premiums. With rising healthcare costs, poor estimations can lead to financial losses or unfair pricing for customers.

Key Insights:

  • Insurance charges were highly skewed — log transformation was applied for normalization.
  • Top predictors: age, BMI, and smoking status (especially smokers with higher costs).
  • Minimal impact from gender or region.

Analytical Approach:

  • Performed EDA with visualizations like histograms, boxplots, and correlation matrices.
  • Selected key features and built a linear regression model on log-transformed charges.
  • Evaluated using R² and MSE to check accuracy and variance explanation.

Solution & Impact: Developed a predictive model that helps insurance firms price policies based on customer characteristics like age, BMI, and smoking status. This leads to smarter risk assessment and premium setting.

Tools: Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn
