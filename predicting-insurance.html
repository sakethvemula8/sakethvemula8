📌 Predicting Insurance Costs with Linear Regression

Built a predictive model to estimate individual medical insurance charges based on demographics and lifestyle factors, using EDA and regression modeling.

📈 Business Problem

Health insurance providers need accurate cost predictions to set fair premiums. Over- or underestimating costs leads to financial losses or dissatisfied customers.

🔍 Key Insights

  • Insurance charges were highly skewed — applied log transformation to normalize.
  • Top predictors: age, BMI, and smoking status.
  • Gender and region showed minimal predictive power.

🧠 Analytical Approach

  • Performed EDA with histograms, boxplots, and a correlation heatmap.
  • Created new feature log_charges to stabilize variance.
  • Built a linear regression model and evaluated performance with R² and MSE.

✅ Solution & Impact

Enabled fairer and more data-driven insurance pricing by predicting costs based on key health and demographic variables. Supports strategic underwriting and pricing.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn
